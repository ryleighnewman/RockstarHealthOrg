How It Fits

It is a press-on upgrade, not an install. Here is the whole process.

1

Measure your stem

Standard power wheelchair joystick stems are about 6.4 mm across. Measure yours with calipers or a ruler before ordering.

2

Press it on

The topper presses firmly onto the stem by hand. No tools, no adhesive, and no dealer appointment.

3

Drive your way

Steer with an open hand, the side of your hand, your wrist, or a light grip. Pop it back off any time.

How It Is Made

Each topper is 3D printed to order from carbon fiber reinforced nylon, an engineering material (PAHT-CF) that is stiff, strong, and light. It stands up to daily use far better than the soft plastic on a stock knob.

We print on a state of the art multi material machine, check quality on every print, and tune each profile for strength and durability. The finish is a clean matte black, and the part is built to last.

Made By A Wheelchair User

This is our first product, designed by a power wheelchair user for wheelchair users, and built by a nonprofit whose goal is comfort and affordability rather than profit.

Questions

Will it fit my joystick?
It fits most standard power wheelchair joystick stems, around 6.4 mm. If you are between sizes or not sure, send us your wheelchair model before you order and we will help you check.
How do I put it on?
It presses onto the stem by hand and holds snug while you drive. Pop it back off whenever you want to swap or clean it.
What does the warranty cover?
An unlimited lifetime warranty, no matter what. If anything ever happens to your topper, or you just need something, reach out and we will take care of you. No time limit, no fine print.
Where does it ship from?
We 3D print every topper in Saint Johns, Florida and ship from there, usually within one to two business days. Shipping is free.
