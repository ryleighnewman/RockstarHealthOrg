About Rockstar Health

A nonprofit-owned mobility company founded by a wheelchair user.

Overview

Rockstar Health is a nonprofit-owned mobility equipment organization focused on redesigning how rehabilitation technology is developed, distributed, and accessed.

The organization was founded in 2026 by Ryleigh Newman, a power wheelchair user with a long-standing personal and academic connection to hereditary neuromuscular disease. After years of firsthand experience navigating the realities of the mobility equipment industry, he started working on a different approach.

Rockstar Health operates through a nonprofit parent organization and a for-profit manufacturing subsidiary. The long-term objective is a commercially sustainable organization whose success directly supports the disability community. See the financial structure.

Our Mission

For decades, the complex rehabilitation wheelchair industry has operated through a fragmented system of manufacturers, dealer networks, proprietary software ecosystems, and reimbursement barriers that often leave users with limited control over the equipment they rely on every day.

Routine adjustments may require dealer appointments. Repairs can take weeks or months. Features are frequently treated as optional upgrades instead of standard accessibility tools. In many cases, mobility depends more on insurance approval than actual need.

Rockstar Health was founded to challenge that structure.

We believe wheelchair users should have more control over their own equipment, more transparency, better access to service and replacement systems, and a more direct relationship with the people designing these products.

We also believe mobility companies should do more than maximize profit. Our long-term goal is to build a structure where commercial success directly supports expanded mobility access for underserved individuals with disabilities.

What We Are Working Toward

Rockstar Health exists to design, manufacture, and distribute mobility equipment for individuals with disabilities, with particular focus on people underserved by the existing system due to cost, geography, or insurance barriers.

  • Structural Price Reduction. We aim to reduce unnecessary costs by simplifying distribution, reducing intermediary dependence, and building around modular manufacturing systems.
  • Financial Assistance. We intend to provide reduced-cost mobility equipment programs for qualifying individuals.
  • Donation Pathways. Future commercial growth and charitable support will help fund mobility equipment access for underserved users.

Meet the Founder

Ryleigh Newman is the founder and CEO of Rockstar Health.

He graduated from the Harriet L. Wilkes Honors College at Florida Atlantic University with a major in Biological and Physical Sciences and concentrations in biochemistry and data science.

His research focuses on hereditary neuromuscular disease, genomics, and rare disease discovery. His honors thesis, Uncovering a Novel Neuromuscular Disorder, investigates a multigenerational hereditary neuropathy using whole genome sequencing and comparative genetic analysis.

As a power wheelchair user himself, his relationship with mobility equipment comes from daily use. Rockstar Health came out of years of watching the gap between what wheelchair users need and what the system actually delivers.

Our Philosophy

Most rehabilitation mobility systems operate through layered dealer networks, proprietary software ecosystems, and reimbursement-driven configuration structures.

Rockstar Health is being built around a different philosophy. We believe:

  • Users should have more control over their equipment
  • Personalization should not require unnecessary gatekeeping
  • Repair systems should prioritize accessibility and speed
  • Warranty systems should not punish self-advocacy
  • Commercial success should support broader mobility access

Rockstar Health is not trying to rebrand what already exists. The structure itself needs to be different.
